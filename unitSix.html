Net U.S. International Investment Position: Frequency [Quarterly]

The Net International Investment Position (IIP) shows the difference between what U.S. residents own abroad and what foreigners own in the U.S. A negative IIP means the U.S. is a net debtor, reflecting years of current account deficits and foreign borrowing.

Nominal Broad U.S. Dollar Index: Frequency [Monthly]

This index measures the U.S. dollar’s strength against a broad set of foreign currencies. An increase in the index means the dollar has appreciated, making imports cheaper but exports less competitive, impacting net exports and GDP.

Exchange Rate: USD to Japanese Yen: Frequency [Daily]

This exchange rate shows how many Japanese yen one U.S. dollar can buy. A rising value means a stronger dollar, which makes Japanese imports cheaper for Americans but can hurt U.S. exports to Japan.

Exchange Rate: USD to Euro: Frequency [Daily]

This graph shows the dollar-to-euro exchange rate. Changes in this rate influence the U.S. trade balance with Europe and are often driven by interest rate differences and economic outlooks between the U.S. and Eurozone.

U.S. Trade Balance: Goods and Services: Frequency [Monthly]

This graph shows the net value of exports minus imports. A trade deficit appears as a negative value and suggests the U.S. is spending more on foreign goods than it earns from exports.

Foreign Holdings of U.S. Federal Debt: Frequency [Quarterly]

This graph shows how much of the U.S. federal debt is owned by foreign governments and institutions. High foreign ownership indicates global trust in the U.S. economy but also exposes fiscal policy to international market dynamics.
