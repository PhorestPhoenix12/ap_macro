US: Real Median Household Income

This graph shows inflation-adjusted median household income in the U.S., reflecting how the typical family’s standard of living has changed over time. It's a key measure of economic well-being and purchasing power for the middle class.

Median Household Income Growth (%) and CPI for Urban Consumers (%)

This dual-axis graph compares the growth rate of household income with the inflation rate (CPI). If income growth lags behind inflation, real purchasing power declines, which affects consumer confidence and consumption levels.

Federal Funds Effective Rate

This graph tracks the federal funds rate — the benchmark interest rate set by the Federal Reserve. Changes in this rate influence household borrowing costs for mortgages, credit cards, and loans, making it highly relevant for everyday financial decisions.
