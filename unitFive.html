Core PCE Price Index: Frequency[Monthly]

The Core PCE Price Index removes food and energy prices to give a clearer picture of long-term inflation trends. Policymakers often use this version to assess whether inflationary pressures are persistent or temporary.

Federal Surplus or Deficit Percent of GDP: Frequency[Quarterly]

This graph shows whether the federal government is running a budget surplus or deficit as a percentage of GDP. It is helpful for analyzing the government’s fiscal stance and the long-term impact of discretionary fiscal policy.

Total Public Debt as Percent of GDP: Frequency[Quarterly]

This graph illustrates the size of the national debt relative to the economy. A rising debt-to-GDP ratio can lead to concerns about sustainability, potential crowding out of private investment, and constraints on future policy options.

Average Hourly Earnings of Employees: Frequency[Monthly]

This graph shows the trend in average hourly wages for U.S. workers in the private sector. Rising wages can signal a tightening labor market, contribute to cost-push inflation, and influence decisions by the Federal Reserve regarding interest rates.

Labor Productivity: Frequency[Quarterly]

Labor productivity measures how much output is produced per hour worked. This graph is essential for understanding long-run economic growth and how increases in productivity shift the long-run aggregate supply (LRAS) curve.

10-Year Treasury Nominal Interest Rate: Frequency[Daily]

This graph shows the nominal interest rate on 10-year government bonds. It reflects long-term expectations of inflation and economic growth and is often referenced in evaluating the stance of monetary policy in the long run.
